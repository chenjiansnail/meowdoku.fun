About Meowdoku

Meowdoku is a free browser logic puzzle that puts a cat-themed spin on the popular "Queens" style of grid puzzle. Every board is divided into colored regions, and your job is to place exactly one cat in every row, every column, and every colored region — with no two cats ever touching, including diagonally. It plays a lot like a cross between Sudoku and a spatial deduction puzzle: there's no luck involved, just careful elimination.

Each puzzle on this page is generated and verified ahead of time to have exactly one valid solution, so if you get stuck, the answer is always findable through logic alone — never guesswork. Boards range from a gentle 5x5 grid up to a tougher 9x9, so you can start easy and work your way up to the expert size. There's nothing to install and nothing to sign up for: pick a size, click a cell, and start solving.

How to Play Meowdoku

  1. Pick a board size from the dropdown above the puzzle — 5x5 is the easiest place to start.
  2. Click any cell to cycle it: empty → ✕ (marked as "not here") → 🐱 (a placed cat) → empty again.
  3. Place exactly one cat in every row, every column, and every colored region.
  4. No two cats can touch, even at the corners — the board will flash red if a placement breaks the rules and cost you a heart.
  5. Use ✕ marks to rule out cells as you eliminate possibilities, the same way you'd pencil-mark a Sudoku grid.
  6. The puzzle is solved the moment every row, column, and region has exactly one correctly placed cat.

Tips & Strategies

  • Start by scanning for any color region that only fits in one or two cells — those are your best opening moves.
  • Once you place a cat, immediately mark ✕ on the rest of its row, column, and region — that's most of your deduction right there.
  • Don't forget the diagonal-touch rule — it's the one most new players miss, and it eliminates more cells than it looks like.
  • If a region's remaining open cells all share the same row or column, that row/column is solved for that region.
  • Stuck? Use the Hint button to reveal one correct placement rather than guessing and losing a heart.
